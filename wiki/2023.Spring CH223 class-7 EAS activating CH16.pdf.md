Activating and Deactivating Effects
• If a substituted benzene derivative reacts more rapidly than benzene
itself, then the substituent is said to be an activating group.
• If a substituted benzene derivative reacts more slowly than benzene
itself, then the substituent is said to be a deactivating group.
1. All meta-directors are deactivating.
2. All ortho-, para-directors (except halogens) are activating.
3. Halogens are deactivating.
