In-class problem
Your synthetic chemist friend is crosslinking polymer A. At some point
during the reaction, the material has passed the gel point. G’ vs
frequency data shows a nice flat line. Now, the theory of rubber
elasticity says that G’=nkT (n=number of crosslinks, k=Boltzmann
constant and T=temperature in deg. K). To test this hypothesis, you
measured G’ of this sample at T1. You then heated the sample to T2
where T2=2T1 (all temps are in deg, K). However, you found that G’ at
T2 is more than twice the G’ at T1. Explain your answer.

In-class problem
A photocrosslinkable system is being crosslinked in the rheometer by
shining UV light. You are interested in monitoring the gelation and
obtaining the gel point by running frequency sweeps at different time
intervals. The problem you have is that the sample gets fully crosslinked
in 20 mins, and running a reasonable frequency sweep takes 10
minutes. Are there any options available for you to monitor this
crosslinking and obtain gel point?
