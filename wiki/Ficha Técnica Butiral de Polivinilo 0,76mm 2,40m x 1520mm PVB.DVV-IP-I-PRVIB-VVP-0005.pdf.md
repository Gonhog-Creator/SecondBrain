Ⅱ. Packing and Transportation.
1. Products marks and packing:
Marks: Serial numbers of product, number of roll, size, guarantee period, date of production, factory name and
contacts.
Packing: PVB film should be separated by PE release film between two layers of PVB film; Vacuum and
aluminium packing into wooden case or paper carton. The qualified products have to match guarantee card,
feedback card and basic information of merchandise.
2. Storage & transportation:
Storage: Products should be stored in clean warehouse, avoid sunlight to irradiate products directly. It should be
stored at temperature of 20℃±5℃, humidity is 20%-30% if package was

opened.

Transportation: No strong sunlight, rain and shake-out while in transportation. The maximum height should no
less than 3 floors.

Ⅱ. The Technical Parameters of PVB Interlayer Film in Producing Process

Step 1: Cutting, Cleaning and Pretreatment.
The cutting trace along the glass border should be proper in order to get a better peeling position. The dimension
of glass panel should be precise, with deviation no more than 2 mm, in case the ragged border causing air
bubbles. After cutting, the glass border should be edged, and cleaned by glass-washing machine. After cleaning,
there should be no contamination like greasy on the glass panels. The endgame of cleaning needs softening
water to rinse, in case of being wasted for lacking of adhesive force. The cleaned glass can only be used after it
is entirely dried and cooled down to normal temperature.
Requirement of Laminating Room:
Environment of laminating room is very important to manufacture laminated glass. Qualified laminating room
should be able to follow the below items.
• The double doors should not open in the meantime and better have air shower equipment in the middle of
double doors.
• The plant temperature should keep on 18-20℃ and humidity 20%-30%.
• Wear antistatic whole suit, glove, headgear.
• Clean the equipment and plant in regular time.
Step 2: Laminating Process.
In a qualified lamination room, evenly spread out PVB film on one glass panel and then assemble two glass
sheets. Use knife to prune and cut off PVB film edges. During the pruning, it is forbidden to forcibly draw the film,
so as to avoid PVB film’s distortion. The border of PVB film should be 2-5 mm longer than that of glass sheets.
And when pruning, the knife should not touch the glass, so as to avoid air bubbles caused by glass particles
around the perimeter of processed laminated glass.
Step 3: Preheating and Exhausting.
The laminated glass needs preheating and exhaust process to press out the leftover air and get a well-edged
sealing. Then it can be pressed with high pressure into the required form. The Pre-Nip Process can be divided
into two methods:
• Hot Roller Exhausting.
Put the warmed-up laminated glass into a calander. After the first rolling, put it into a thermostat container. After
the second rolling, exhaust the air and seal the edges then finish this process. After the second rolling, seal a
transparent band around the laminated glass which well-seals the edge in case the air in the high pressure
autoclave should stream back and cause air bubbles. Nontransparent areas distributed evenly in the other parts
are allowable. This method of using single thermostat container and double rubber roller squeezer requires
strictly-controlled temperature on the surface of the glass. It can neither be too high (in case it makes the edges
sealing too early to allow the interior air exhaust successfully), nor too low (in case it makes the edge sealing
imperfect which causes back-steaming air bubbles).
Suggestion: Keep the surface temperature of the laminated glass within 25-40℃. The space of the first
rubber-roller squeezing process should be about 1 mm less than the laminated glass' thickness. The pressure
should between 0.2-0.5MPa. The second roller squeezing process should be about 2mm less than the laminated
glass' thickness, and the pressure is 0.3-0.5Mpa. For the second roller squeezing, the surface temperature of the
glass must be strictly controlled within 60-80℃. When manufacturing, prepare all the glass in advance and try the
best to do the process continuously and avoid long waiting time intervals, in case the temperature in the
thermo-stated container changes too much so that the surface temperature cannot be controlled precisely.

• Vacuum Exhausting.
Put the laminated glass into a vacuum bag or vacuum rubber gasket. With cool pumping and heat pumping,
exhaust the air between glass and PVB film layers by the pressure difference and seal the edges.
Suggestion: Decompression vacuum degree should beyond 650 mmHg, the temperature of cool pumping
should be under 25℃, and the time should be more than 15 minutes. During heat pumping, the surface
temperature of the glass should reach 70-120℃ and be kept for more than 30 minutes.
Step 4: Autoclaving Process.
Put the air-exhausted glass bracket vertically, or with an angle less than 10°. The distance between laminated
glass should be more than 5 mm. Close the door after the laminated glass is put into the autoclave, and first raise
the temperature to 45℃, then raise the temperature and the pressure at the same time. Control the temperature
rising speed at 5℃ per minute, and the pressure adding speed at 0.06 Mpa per minute. When the temperature
reaches 120-135℃ and pressure up to 1.0-1.5Mpa, maintain the same for 30 to 60 minutes. After that, keep the
same pressure and lower the temperature to 45℃, then lower pressure to atmospheric pressure. This finishes the
process of autoclaving and the laminated glass is ready. The temperature must be controlled strictly when rising
and lowering pressure in case of air bubbles in the edge of laminated glass.
