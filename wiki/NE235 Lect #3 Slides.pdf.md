Neutron Reactions in a Fission Reactor:
• Absorption reactions: Capture, Fission, Breeding
• Scattering reactions: Moderation, Leakage, Reflection
Radioactive materials in reactor lab:
• Reactor Fuel – fission g’s & 1no’s, fission products (135I, 90Sr, 140La, etc…)
• Activation Products – 24Na in coolant, 28Al in core structures, 41Ar in air
• Sealed Isotopic Sources - 60Co, 137Cs, 241Am, 239Pu-Be

Radio - “Activity”
The rate at which radioactive material is decaying
and releasing ionizing radiation is the “Activity”.
Units of Activity
• Becquerel (Bq):
1 Bq = 1 DPS (decays per second)

• Curie (Ci), mCi, Ci:
1 Ci = 3.7 X 1010 DPS = Activity in 1 gram of Radium

Radioactive Decay Equation

A(t) = A o e -t
Where: Ao = Initial Activity (DPS or Curies)
A(t) = Activity after decay time “t”
= ln(2) / T1/2
T1/2 = isotopic half life
 The amount of radioactive material (activity A) decreases
over time at a rate depending on the half life (T1/2) of the
decaying isotope.

Biological Effects of Ionizing Radiation
Ionization disrupts molecular bonds in cells & tissues

• Rapidly reproducing cells at
greatest risk:
– Bone marrow
– Fetus/Reproductive cells
– Skin cells
– Gastrointestinal tract
– Lining of Lungs

• Highly specialized cells less
at risk:
– Muscles
– Nerves

Biological Effects
Human data from WWII atomic bomb survivors and radiation accidents (e.g.
weapons complex, Chernobyl)
• Non-Stochastic (deterministic) effects - Severity increases with dose
– Skin reddening (burns)
– Sterility
– Acute Radiation Syndrome (ARS) (>150 REM):
Hematopoietic Syndrome – damage to blood & blood forming organs @ 1001000 REM. White blood cells and blood forming stem cells in bone marrow
are most sensitive and die. Infections, lack of oxygen transfer result.
➢ Gastrointestinal Syndrome – damage to lining of stomach & intestines @
1000-5000 REM. Lining of GI tract degrades/dies resulting in dehydration and
blood poisoning.
➢ Central nervous system – breakdown of nerve cell function. @ Doses > 5000
REM, death within a few hours due to fluid accumulation on brain.
➢

• Stochastic effects - Probability of effect increases with dose:
– Cancer
– Genetic Mutations

Radiation Safety: Regs & Limits
Based on Linear No-Threshold Theory
ALARA – Keep dose As Low As Reasonably Achievable
Where possible: Reduce amount of radioactivity,
minimize time near source, maximize distance from
source, and utilize appropriate shielding.
10CFR20 – NRC Radiation Protection Limits (link)
• Radiation Workers :
✓5 REM Whole Body
✓15 REM to Eye
✓50 REM to extremities
• Public: 100 mREM/Year; 2 mREM in 1 hour
• ALARA limits – 10% of NRC Limits

Summary
1. Nuclear reactions occur inside reactor which release
radiation, either promptly or through decay of
radioactive products.
2. Nuclear radiation (a,b,g,n) emitted have different
ranges and interactions with matter.
3. Biological radiation dose & resulting biological effects
are dependent on type of radiation, duration and
amount of exposure.

4. 10CFR20 provides regulatory limits for radiation
exposure – keep doses ALARA.
5. Health risk is a function of biological dose.
