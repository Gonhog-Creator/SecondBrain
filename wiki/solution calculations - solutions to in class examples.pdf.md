Units of concentration
Equivalent units of molarity
M = mol/L = mmol/mL
mM = mmol/L = µmol/mL
µM = µmol/L = nmol/mL
Example: A solution was prepared by adding 600.0 µmoles
of solute to 2.00 mL of solution. Express its concentration
in M
SOLUTION:
(600.0µmol/2.00mL)*(1mmol /10 3µmol) = 0.300 M

Units of concentration
Formality, F
(Skoog: analytical Molarity)

F = (Total moles solutes used to prepare a solution)/L solution
Non electrolyte M = F.
Weak electrolyte M < F.
Strong electrolyte M = 0 for the molecular form.

Definition & equivalent units of w/v
For dilute aqueous solutions, density ~ 1
i.e. 1 g solution = 1 mL solutions.
µg/mL = µg/g = w/w ppm = w/v ppm

w/v % = g/100 mL = kg/100 L = g/dL
w/v ppm = g/106 mL = µg/mL = mg/L
w/v ppb = g/109 mL = ng/mL = µg/L
1 cm3 = 1 cc = 1 mL
sometimes %, ppm, and ppb are used without displaying w/v when reporting
concentration of solid solute in liquid solution. Example: NaCl in sea water, Ca in milk
Example: for a dilute aqueous solutions (d = 1g/mL)
1ppm(w/w) = (1 µg solute/g solution) x (1 g solution/mL solution) = (1 µg solute/mL solution)
= 1ppm (w/v)

Example 4
It is recommended that drinking water contain 1.6 ppm
fluoride (F-) for prevention of tooth decay. A certain
reservoir contained an estimated 1.59 x 1012 mL water.
How many kg of NaF should be added to give 1.6 ppm F- ?
(fw NaF =41.988 , fw F= 18.998)
SOLUTION:
(1.6 µg F-/mL solution)*(1.59x1012 mL)*(41.988µg NaF/18.998 µg F-)*(1kg/109µg)
