LAB MODULE #3 - PULSTAR REACTOR STARTUP
Objective: Predict and observe reactor behavior while performing a routine reactor
startup. Take the reactor from a shutdown state through sub-critical
multiplication and to super-criticality. Establish critical rod positions.
Characteristics of SUB-CRITICAL MULTIPLICATION:
•

Doubling the Source Range Monitor (SRM) count-rate halves the difference between the
current value for Keff and 1, therefore taking the reactor halfway to criticality.

•

Withdraw the control rods and double the SRM count-rate approximately 5 times to
bring the reactor to criticality (or just super-critical).

•

The closer you get to criticality, the longer it takes for the SRM count rate to reach
subcritical equilibrium (i.e. a stable non-increasing neutron population).

Characteristics of a SUPER-CRITICAL REACTOR:
•

Linearly increasing traces on the SRM and Log-N semi-log plot recorder traces (without
control rod motion) indicates an exponential increase in the reactor neutron population.
This is an indication that the reactor is SUPER-CRITICAL.

•

A Positive Stable Period (τ ) (without control rod motion) as indicated on the SRM and
Log-N Channel meters confirms an exponential increase in neutron population. This is
another indication that the reactor is SUPER-CRITICAL.

Institute for Nuclear Power Operations (INPO) - Guidelines for Reactor Operations:
a) STOP & THINK – what operations evolution/procedure is being performed?
b) TOUCH – which control does the procedural step indicate should be manipulated?
c) VERIFY – am I about to manipulate the correct control?
d) ANTICIPATE – how should the reactor systems respond to the manipulation?
e) MANIPULATE – perform the control manipulation.
f) OBSERVE – observe the change in reactor behavior in response to the control manipulation.
Finally, (RE-)VERIFY that the reactor behavior you OBSERVED is what you ANTICIPATED!
Otherwise – SCRAM!!
NOTE:

The responses of the reactor systems to correct manipulations of the
controls are FULLY UNDERSTOOD and are ENTIRELY PREDICTABLE!

Useful Definitions:
•

CRITICALITY – Neutron population is stable and maintained by fission; where Keff = 1; ρ = 0

•

SUB-CRITICALITY – neutron population is decreasing; where 0 < Keff < 1; ρ < 0

•

SUPER-CRITICALITY – neutron population is increasing exponentially due to an increasing
fission rate; where Keff > 1; ρ > 0

•

REACTOR PERIOD (τ)(SEC) – The time in seconds it takes for reactor power to change by a
factor of ‘e’ (i.e. 2.718). A small value indicates power is changing quickly; a large or
infinite value indicates power is changing slowly over time or not at all.

•
