Limitations to Beer’s law
Beer’s law is applicable mainly at low conc.
At high concentrations:
(1) interactions between particles alters charge distribution either in
the absorbing species or excited species or both and thus changes
the energy needed for excitation
(2) refractive index of the medium may change.
Other conditions that might cause Beer’s law to ‘apparently” fail, even
under low concentrations:

• Chemical
• Instrumental

G. Rabah CH315

Limitations to Beer’s law
Chemical:
• Interactions between absorbing species that will affect their charge
distributions
• Association, dissociation or reaction of the absorbing species with the
solvent to generate a product that has a different absorption spectrum
from analyte
• Incomplete reaction with color developing reagent: Keq is too small, or
too little color developing reagent
• Photodecomposition of the sample
Instrumental:
• non-monochromatic (polychromatic) light
• stray light

solution was transferred to a 100-mL volumetric flask and diluted to volume

absorption line) by flame atomic absorption; a net absorbance of 0.119 was
observed. A 2.00 mM standard Cu solution yielded a net absorbance of 0.125
on the same AA instrument.
Determine the %Cu in the alloy.
