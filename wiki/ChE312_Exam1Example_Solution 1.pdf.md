Prob. 1 (10 pts). Define "species volatility" and "relative volatility" for vapor-liquid equilibrium, both
in words and with mole-fraction variables. Are these definitions valid for non-ideal gases and liquids?
Volatility (or distribution coefficient 𝐾! ) is 𝑦! /𝑥! . For a chemical species, its volatility is how high its
mole fraction is in the gas phase relative to the liquid phase. If it is highly volatile, 𝑦! >> 𝑥! , and if its
volatility is low, 𝑦! << 𝑥! .
Relative volatility is 𝛼"# = (𝑦" /𝑥" )/(𝑦# /𝑥# ). It compares volatility of species 1 relative to some
reference species 2, usually choosing the most-volatile species as #1.
Yes, these definitions valid for non-ideal gases and liquids because they make no assumptions of ideality.
[Remark: If a phase is nonideal, the same compound may not always be more volatile, such as happens
for an azeotrope.]

Prob. 2 (5 pts). To make a distillation column work, what is the purpose of a condenser at the top?
The condenser generates liquid to be refluxed back into the column, providing liquid for vapor-liquid
countercontacting.
[Liquid or saturated vapor may be generated by the condenser.]

Prob. 3 (5 pts). To make a distillation column work, what is the purpose of a reboiler at the bottom?
The reboiler generates vapor to feed into the bottom of the column, providing vapor for vapor-liquid
countercontacting.
