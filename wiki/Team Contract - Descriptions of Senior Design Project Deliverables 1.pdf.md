Description of Senior Design Project Deliverables Submitted in CHE 450
There are four deliverables assigned in CHE 450 that are related to a group’s capstone project.
All documents should be single-spaced, and figures/tables should be sized for ease of reading.
Writing should be clear and concise. Submission will be electronic, so as much as possible,
maintain your references in pdf form. All deliverables will be submitted to TurnItIn.com, and all
deliverables other than the Team Contract will be submitted to your project mentor as well. All
team members should be willing to be listed as authors on documents.
Team Contract: Electronic submission to TurnItIn.com and share via email or other agreedupon means with mentor(s). The team contract is a management tool designed to facilitate the
smooth operation of your team and avoid any confusion regarding expectations. The team
contract is your team’s first attempt to clearly communicate the operating philosophy of your
group to the outside world and, more importantly, to group members themselves. In this
document you will set out the basic operating rules for the group. It is important for each team
member to understand their own strengths and weaknesses, and those of their teammates. A
personal inventory of abilities (what do I do well, what do I dislike doing, what productive and
counterproductive habits do I exhibit?) will go a long way to helping you recognize what you
have to offer to the team, and what personal and professional development opportunities might
be available through your group activities.
Develop and submit a team contract that explicitly includes the following material:
a) Guiding Principles: One to two paragraphs putting forward your group’s overall code of
conduct.
b) Mission Statement: ~ 25 word statement related to guiding principles and the preliminary
focus.
c) Meeting Management: This section explicitly lays out how meetings will be scheduled and
managed. Be clear about how far in advance the meetings will be scheduled and the agenda
developed/provided, meeting frequency, and how disagreements will be managed within the
meetings. This includes both team meetings and meetings with mentor(s).
d) Accountability Process: It is vitally important for the team to define what success looks
like and establish a process for peer support and internal review (in addition to establishing
expectations for CATME evaluations). Since communication is a key element of
accountability, this section should also describe the communication strategies and tools that
your group intends to implement, both within the team and with the mentor(s). Expectations
should be given for member behavior regarding common sticking points, including (1)
members arriving late or missing meetings, (2) members late or not completing assignments,
(3) members not participating in meetings (e.g. spending time on their phone or online for
activities not related to the project), (4) policies for working late (i.e. “I’m too tired, do you
mind finishing things?”), plus at least one other problem your group can think of that is not
suggested here.
