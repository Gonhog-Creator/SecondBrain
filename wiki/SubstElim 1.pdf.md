The following page provides a flow chart used to decide what type of reaction occurs based
upon reaction conditions.

The flow chart above is designed to bring it all together for you, conceptually. The first step is to
designate the degree of substitution of the substrate then assess the strength of the base and if
needed the protic or aprotic nature of the solvent.
*The strength of the base usually determines the order of the reaction. If a strong base is
present, second order kinetics follow (SN2 or E2). If no strong base or strong nucleophile are
present, then a mixture of SN1/E1 occurs.
**Some nucleophiles and bases favor SN or E chemistry. To promote elimination, often times
bulky bases (like (CH3)3CO – ) are used and can result in formation of the least substituted
alkene. To promote substitution with 2o substrates, good nucleophiles which are weak bases
are used (RS-, NC-, I-, Br-, N3-).
