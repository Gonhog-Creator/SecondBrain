6. Course (Catalog) Description: Laboratory experiments in unit operations of heat transfer and
fluid flow. Laboratory safety, technical report writing, statistics, experimental design, error
analysis and instrumentation.
7. Prerequisite(s): Prerequisite = CHE 311
8. Textbook(s) and/or Other Required Material: CHE 330 Coursepack (available free on Moodle)
9. Course Learning Objectives:
1) Produce written technical reports as part of an engineering team.
2) Orally present technical information as part of an engineering team.
3) Capably perform hands-on engineering as part of an engineering team.
4) Solve problems using statistics relevant to engineering.
5) Design and analyze an experiment using factorial experimental design principles.
6) Identify causes and preventative measures for process safety incidents.
10. Class/Laboratory Schedule (# sessions/week and session duration): Two 50-minute lectures
per week; one 4-hour lab period per week. A total of four experiments are spread over the
semester. Each experiment is preceded by an online preview session and followed by a
period for data analysis and preparation of report and/or presentation prior to delivery of
materials based on technical analysis of the experiment.
11. Contribution of the Course to Meeting the Professional Component: Engineering Topics (4
semester hours) with design content

Academic Integrity: Students are required to comply with the university policy on
academic integrity found in the Code of Student Conduct found at http://policies.ncsu.
edu/policy/pol-11-35-01. It is the instructorвАЩs understanding and expectation that the
student's signature on any test or assignment means that the student contributed to the
assignment in question (if a group assignment) and that they neither gave nor received
unauthorized aid. Authorized aid on an individual assignment includes discussing the
interpretation of the problem statement, sharing ideas or approaches for solving the
problem, and explaining concepts involved in the problem. Any other aid would be
unauthorized and a violation of the academic integrity policy. Unauthorized aid
additionally includes accessing on-line solutions, whether that be complete or partial online copies of textbook problem solutions or paying someone to complete your homework.
In addition, any computer work submitted must be completed on your own personal
computer or from your own @ncsu.edu account to avoid confusion about the origin of the
file, and no sharing of files in any way is allowed. All cases of academic misconduct will
be submitted to the Office of Student Conduct. Students found guilty of academic
misconduct will be subject to, at a minimum, a zero on the assignment in question, up to a
zero for that course component (e.g. a zero for the homework portion of the final grade), or
a failing grade in the course, depending on the nature of the violation. In addition, if you
are found guilty of academic misconduct in the course, you will be on academic integrity
probation for the remainder of your years at NCSU and may be required to report your
violation on future professional school applications.
