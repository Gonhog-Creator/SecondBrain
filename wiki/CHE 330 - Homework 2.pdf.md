DEPARTMENT OF CHEMICAL & BIOMOLECULAR ENGINEERING
NORTH CAROLINA STATE UNIVERSITY
CHE 330
Homework Set 2
Problem 1 (30%) A manufacturer of metal pistons finds that on average, 12% of pistons
produced at the facility are rejected because they are either oversize or undersize. Determine the
probability that a production batch of 10 pistons will contain (a, 10%) exactly 2 rejects, (b,
10%) no more than 2 rejects, (c, 10%) at least 2 rejects.
Problem 2 (30%) Two fair dice each have 5 sides numbered 1-5. Let X be the sum of the two
dice. Determine (a, 10%) the probability distribution of X and verify that the sum of all
probabilities is equal to 1, (b, 10%) the expectation value of X and (c, 10%) the standard
deviation of X.
Problem 3 (30%) The number of infections X in a hospital each week has been shown to follow
a Poisson distribution with mean 3.0 infections per week. Calculate the following probabilities:
(a, 5%) P(X = 0), (b, 10%) P(X < 4), (c, 10%) P(X > 9). (d, 5%) Suppose there are 9 infections
at the hospital next week. Would you consider this normal? Why?
Problem 4 (10%) A roulette wheel has landed on “red” for each of the past 10 spins. A friend
comments that it would be wise to wager on “black” immediately, correctly stating that the odds
of the roulette wheel landing on “red” 11 times in a row are below 1%. What is the technical
error in this line of thought?
