1. (51 pts total) Ammonia (NH3) is formed from N2 and H2 in a continuous steady-state reactionseparation process. The fresh feed to the process has a flow rate of 224 L(STP)/s and contains 25.0
mol% N2 and the balance H2. This stream is joined by a recycle stream which contains
24.8 mol% N2, 74.4 mol% H2, and balance NH3. The combined stream flows into the reactor. The
reactor product stream, which contains all three species, passes through a separation unit in which
95.2 % of the ammonia (molar flow rate) is collected as the product stream leaving the overall
process. The gas stream leaving the separation unit is recycled back into the system.

(a) (10 pts) Re-draw the given flow chart, completely label all process streams, and identify the
basis of calculation.
(b) (4 pts) In terms of your labeled variables, write expressions for the overall and single-pass
conversions of N2. DO NOT perform any calculations.
(c) (4 pts) Do the degree-of-freedom (DOF) analysis for the overall process using atomic
balances.
Hint: Note that you are only allowed one atomic balance for the overall process.
(d) (3 pts) Explain why only 1 atomic balance is allowed for the overall process.
(e) (4 pts) Calculate the production rate of ammonia (mol/s)
(f) (15 pts) Do additional DOF analyses on the three subsystems shown on the flow chart and
determine an efficient order in which the subsystems should be analyzed to determine all
remaining unknown variables on the chart.
Hint: Remember to use molecular balances for non-reactive subsystems.
(g) (8 pts) Based on your analysis in Part (f), write the complete set of equations you would
solve to calculate the recycle stream flow rate (mol/s). You should have n equations and n
unknowns. Do NOT perform any calculations. DO NOT write more than you need to solve
for the recycle flow rate.
(h) (3 pts) Suppose the feed to the process contained trace amounts of argon, an inert gas.
Why and how would the process have to be modified?

2. (12 pts total) A gas separation unit is used to separate oxygen from nitrogen in the air. Prior to
separation, air is compressed to 10 atm in a multi-stage compressor and then cooled to -136.6°C.
The air flowrate is 200 SCMM (Standard Cubic Meter per Minute). Calculate the volumetric flow rate
of air in cubic meters per minute (m3/min) after it has been compressed and cooled using:
(a) (3 pts) The ideal gas equation of state
(b) (7 pts) Kay’s rule and the compressibility factor equation of state
(c) (2 pts) Is the ideal gas equation of state a good approximation under these conditions?
Briefly justify your answer.

emerge from the furnace containing 10 mol/s CO. The conversion of C3H8 is 90%. The flowsheet is
provided below:

(a) (4 pts) Write out and balance the relevant reaction(s) for this system.
(b) (6 pts) Do a degree-of-freedom analysis around the furnace using the atomic balance
approach.
(c) (2 pts) Can you assume that the flammable gas behaves as an ideal gas? Justify your
answer.
