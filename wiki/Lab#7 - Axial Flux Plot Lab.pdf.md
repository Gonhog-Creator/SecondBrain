PULSTAR REACTOR LABORATORIES

LAB MODULE #7: AXIAL FLUX PLOT LAB
LABORATORY OBJECTIVES:
1. Measure operational I-vs-V curve for miniature in-core (MIC) fission chamber detector and
determine operational voltage plateau.
2. Verify linearity of detection channel response relative to neutron flux.
3. Measure the axial neutron flux profile in a reactor fuel assembly cooling channel and calculate
the power peaking factor.
4. Observe shadowing of ex-core instrumentation channels through banking control rods.

PRECONDITIONS:
1. Reactor Daily Startup Checklist completed and signed by Designated Senior Reactor Operator
(DSRO).
2. Reactor startup has been authorized by DSRO.
3. Licensed Reactor Operator is on duty at the reactor control console.
4. Instrument well inserted into fuel assembly adjacent to control rod.

6. Verify linearity of MIC response by checking scaling factors between levels.
7. Vary reactor power to obtain an on-scale reading for the ammeter with MIC fully inserted in
instrumentation wand.
8. Measure the cooling channel axial flux profile by withdrawing the MIC at a fixed rate and
plotting the voltage –vs- time curve using the data acquisition system – see Figure #4.
9. The nominal sensitivity of the MIC is ~1.5x10-17 Amps/neutrons/cm2/sec (or A/nv). This may be
utilized along with the axial profile data to determine the neutron flux as a function of height in
the reactor core.
