Jose Barbeito
9/7/22
Problem set 2, question 1
After taking my test, I learned much about how I prefer to learn in a classroom setting. The results
that I received, a 3 for Active/Reflective, a 1 for Sensing/Intuitive, a 9 for Visual/Verbal, and a 9 for
Sequential/Global, tell me the type of learner I am: an energetic, visual learner who would prefer to
learn a process step by step. Looking into each of these four categories, I can further understand my
preferences and change my actions within class to better suit my learning style.
My Active/Reflective score was a 3 (leaning active), which makes a lot of sense to me. I prefer being
active in the way I think and interact with the class and others, but I also put a lot of effort into
thinking deeply about certain topics on my own time. This balances out the two sides, but I lean more
towards the active side because I'm a naturally energetic person, especially in the classroom setting. I’m
excited to see what other classmates have an Active learning style, because I would prefer them in
group works. I thoroughly enjoy working in a group where people are energetic, constantly bouncing ideas
off one another.
My Sensing/Intuitive score was a 1 (leaning sensing). This one surprised me, but upon further reflection,
makes a lot of sense. Being an engineer, I love order and straightforward processes. I much rather
prefer a set way of doing things rather than being thrust upon a question and told to struggle with it
until I figure it out. For scenarios like the latter, I end up remembering more the harsh struggle and my
negative feeling about it than the actual process and the end result. This in turn means I end up not
knowing how to apply the theory to another similar yet different problem. This observation would explain
why I lean more Sensing than Intuitive. I believe that what balances out that side of me is a creative
facet that I have cultivated throughout my life. I love creating things and am constantly searching for a
creative outlet, whatever that may be. This, I believe, is the reason for my “intuitive” side, as most of
the questions were geared towards your creative freedom within a scientific context, while I answered
about my creative intuition within a life context.
The next category is no surprise. In the Visual/Verbal section, I am a 9 (leaning visual). I despise only
listening to something being explained, and would much rather see it with my own eyes. Many times I
have been able to copy someone's actions - whether it be a sport or a method of solving a problem simply by observing them do it. I learn visually, and seeing charts and diagrams helps me to learn the
subject matter tremendously.
The last section, Sequential/Global, gave me a score of 9 (leaning sequential). I had never heard of this
category before, and learning that I was a sequential learner resonated true with me. Giving me a vague
understanding of how the whole works does not help my brain to understand it. I am very easily able to
match pieces together and see how they work in conjunction when I fully understand each detail. I refuse to
leave any detail about the part unnoticed or misunderstood, and this usually leads me to either have an
immense amount of knowledge about a very distinct part of the process or getting easily bogged down in the
wrong details. It has helped me in the past and has hurt me as well.

Overall, this learning test was helpful. It gave me a new facet to see the way I learn and will surely help
me understand how to be a better engineering student in the future at NC State.
