Jose Maria Barbeito
8/27/22
Autobiography
As the oldest of five, I was brought up with a large amount of responsibility. I’ve taken
many roles, including handyman, counselor, peacemaker, and leader, and learned to live in the
chaos of a seven-person family. Being raised on a farm, I’ve had to make, fix, and design
everything imaginable. I’ve built sheds and fences, fixed chicken coops and air conditioning
units, re-routed plumbing and re-wired light switches; I’ve kept bees (still do!), skinned rabbits
and delivered lambs on a cold February morning.
My parents immigrated from Argentina and brought with them a culture and heritage that
will stay with me. My family has been immersed in a diverse local community that includes
families from all over the world. Two years ago, I traveled alone to Argentina to work on a cattle
ranch, an experience I will never forget. There, I worked and rode alongside gauchos eight
hours a day, vaccinating cattle and sharing fireside country asados; this opportunity allowed me
to understand and appreciate my heritage like never before.
This past summer, I traveled the world. Through 18 flights, countless hours within
busses, and many many car trips, I visited five different countries, seeing the vast Australian
outback, the Swiss Alps, the French countryside, the Great Barrier Reef, the Highlands of
Scotland, and sprawling cities such as London and Paris. The experience as a whole gave me
the opportunity to grow as a person and discover more about myself.
I love doing chemistry and using engineering to do fun projects. I am super excited for
CHE 205 because it is the perfect class to experience what it would be like to use engineering
and chemistry together in real life scenarios.
