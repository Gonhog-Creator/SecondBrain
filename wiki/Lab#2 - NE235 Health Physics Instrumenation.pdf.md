GM Detectors
яБ░

A fill gas (typically a mixture of Argon and Methane) is
contained in a sealed tube of various shapes, sizes

яБ░

Ionization occurs in the fill gas and in the detector wall
material (wall is at ground potential and serves as the
cathode)

яБ░

GM detectors produce a full amplitude pulse (count) for any
ion that enters the detector (no energy distinction).

яБ░

Used to detect alpha, beta, gamma and x-rays

яБ░

Used primarily for contamination surveys, reading out as a
count rate (CPM).

яБ░

May be calibrated to give approximate exposure rate

Ion Chambers
яБ░

Fill gas is air that may or may not be contained in a
sealed chamber

яБ░

Ionization occurs in the fill gas and in the detector wall
material (wall is at ground potential and serves as the
cathode)

яБ░

Ions in the fill gas are collected at the detector anode
and produce a pulse height proportional to the number
of interactions, or ionization, occurring in the detection
volume

яБ░

Ion chambers are very accurate and allow distinction
between radiation types based on pulse height.

яБ░

Used to detect beta, gamma and x-rays

яБ░

Used to accurately measure exposure rate (mR/hr)
for external radiation fields.

deposition rate.
