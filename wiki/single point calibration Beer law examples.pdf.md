Beer’s law single point calibration – Practice example 1
A 4.629 g sample of an alloy was dissolved in nitric acid; the resulting solution was
transferred to a 100-mL volumetric flask and diluted to volume with water.
A 5.0 mL portion of the solution was pipetted into a 50 mL volumetric flask and
diluted to volume with water. This solution was analyzed at 324.7 nm (Cu absorption
line) by flame atomic absorption; a net absorbance of 0.119 was observed. A 2.00 mM
standard Cu solution yielded a net absorbance of 0.125 on the same AA instrument.
Determine the %Cu in the alloy.

Beer’s law single point calibration – Practice example 2
A 10-mL aliquot of a 6.80 x 10-4 M Fe3+ was pipetted into a 50-mL volumetric flask,
acidified with nitric acid and treated with excess KSCN to form a red complex with
SCN- ion before diluting to volume with deionized water. The resulting solution
transmitted 39.1 % of the light in a 1.00 cm cell at 447 nm, which is a wavelength
near the absorption peak for the complex.
A 25.0 mL sample of runoff water was acidified with nitric acid and treated with
excess KSCN to form a red complex. The solution was diluted to 100.0 mL in a
volumetric flask. The sample was found to transmit 67.8 % of the light in a 1.00 cm
cell at the same wavelength. Calculate the concentration of Fe3+ in the runoff water.
