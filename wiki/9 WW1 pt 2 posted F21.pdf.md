Flu v Covid
• The waves of the flu seem to have been less varied than the waves of
Covid. Therefore, it is easier to generalize about the flu than about Covid.
The first “original” variant of Covid and the Delta variant are very different
from the Omicron variant.
• Because Flu symptoms (in general) faster to emerge than Covid symptoms,
many people who can spread Covid have been asymptomatic.
• Spread in similar ways; flu seems to have been more contagious than
Original and delta, but not Omicron (?)
• Spanish Flu hit young people (ages 20-40) hardest (ordinary flu doesn’t);
Covid hits the old hardest. True even now, although rates among young
with Omicron are rising.
• Covid and flu share complications – but Covid seems to have many more,
still mysterious (eg rare Kawasaki-like in children)
• Spanish flu was more deadly – perhaps due to Covid medicine (Covid
deaths worldwide [Jan. 2022] = 5.63 million; comparable deaths today of
Spanish flu = ~320 million globally)
