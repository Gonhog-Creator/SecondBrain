1. Consider the chemical vapor deposition of silicon nitride (Si3N4) by the following reaction:
3𝑆𝑖𝐻4(𝑔) + 4𝑁𝐻3(𝑔) ↔ 𝑆𝑖3 𝑁4(𝑠) + 12𝐻2(𝑔)
a) On the same graph, make a schematic plot of the natural logarithm of the Si3N4 growth
rate versus 1/T (K-1) for each of the following three growth processes and identify each
on the plot:
a. A surface-reaction controlled process
b. A gas diffusion controlled process
c. The actual rate-limiting rate when both are occurring in series
b) Explain why each plot has the temperature dependence shown.
c) Make a plot of the SiH4 gas pressure as a function of distance away from the growing
silicon nitride film surface for the following three cases:
a. Surface reaction control
b. Gas diffusion control
c. When both processes have about the same rate
Clearly identify each case and indicate important distances and pressures. Assume that
the gases are flowing over a flat substrate on which the deposition is being made.
2. A vacuum reactor is used to synthesize WO 3 nanoparticles for use in an electrochromic
window device. To create the WO3 nanoparticles, a tungsten (W) metal filament 1.0 mm
in diameter and 10 cm long is heated to 2,200°C in a vacuum with a trace pressure of
oxygen gas. Assuming all of the W evaporating from the surface of the filament is rapidly
and completely oxidized to WO3, determine the maximum rate of production of WO3 from
this reactor (g/s). The equilibrium vapor pressure of W at 2,200°C is approximately 10-10
atm.
3. Titanium undergoes active gas corrosion in HCl according to the reaction
𝑇𝑖(𝑠) + 4𝐻𝐶𝑙(𝑔) ↔ 𝑇𝑖𝐶𝑙4(𝑔) + 2𝐻2(𝑔)
The rate of etching of the Ti surface is found to be 2.88 𝜇m/min. The following additional
information is provided:
• k0 = 1.2 x 102 cm/s
• PHCl = 0.10 atm
• MTi = 48 g/mol
• 𝜌𝑇𝑖 = 4.5 g/cm3
• T = 1327°C
a) Calculate the rate constant for the reaction.
b) Calculate the activation energy for the reaction.
c) What is the time required to remove 5 mm of Ti?
