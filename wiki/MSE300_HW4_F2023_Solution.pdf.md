b) Determine the general coordinates of the point group 4mm based on your

4mm is a tetragonal point group consisting of a tetrad along z, mirror planes
normal to x and y axes and mirror planes normal to the axes bisecting the x
and y axes.
We can use a mirror plane normal to the x axis and going through the
center of the point group to reflect all 4 points, which changes the sign in
front of the first coordinate:
1 (x, y, z) в†’ 5 (-x, y, z)
2 (y, -x, z) в†’ 6 (-y, -x, z)
3 (-x, -y, z) в†’ (-(-x), -y, z) = 7 (x, -y, z)
4 (-y, x, z) в†’ (-(-y), x, z) = 8 (y, x, z)
We now have 8 points and no duplicates.
Next, we can use the other mirror plane (normal to the y axis) with a few of
these coordinates to see whether additional points are generated or
duplicates are formed:

e) Are the general positions of this group occupied?
Solution: This requires knowledge about the order of the space group and the
multiplicity of the most general coordinate.
In this case, O is in 16f, which includes the coordinate (x,y,z), which is the most
general coordinate. I41/a takes (x,y,z) and applies a screw tetrad, which creates 4
distinct general coordinates. Assuming these do not overlap with the glade plane,
the latter produces 4 additional points. The centering then takes all 8 and adds
1/2,1/2,1/2 to create 16 general coordinates. This is the order of the group and
cannot be surpassed, hence the answer is yes, the general coordinates of the
space group are occupied by O.
f) What is the point symmetry of the site occupied by W?
Answer: Normally, general coordinates do not sit on point symmetries. However,
W occupies the 4a sites. The Wykoff letter is the lowest in the alphabet, therefore
W occupies a site that corresponds with the highest symmetry of the space group.
In I41/a, the screw tetrad is the highest symmetry. Therefore all W atoms are on a
screw tetrad.
Alternatively, one can answer that in a tetragonal system the tetrad (and screw
tetrad) is always along the c-axis and centered on the unit cellвЂ™s a-b plane. Given
the coordinates of W include the body center, namely, (1/2,1/2,1/2), this means
that sites occupied by W have the screw tetrad.
