DEPARTMENT OF CHEMICAL & BIOMOLECULAR ENGINEERING
NORTH CAROLINA STATE UNIVERSITY
CHE 330
Homework Set 4
Problem 1 (20%) A company claims that using their specialty vacuum will “significantly
reduce” the time needed to vacuum a 2000 ft2 home. Assume it is well-established that it takes
45 minutes to vacuum a 2000 ft2 home, with a standard deviation of 4.2 minutes. Utilizing the
specialty vacuum in 9 separate tests demonstrates that the specialty vacuum takes an average of
41.5 minutes to vacuum a 2000 ft2 home. Is the company’s claim justified at the (a, 10%) 95%
confidence level? (b, 10%) 99% confidence level?
Problem 2 (30%) You are a quality control engineer at a popcorn company claiming that its
bags of microwaveable popcorn contain a mean of 1250 kernels with a standard deviation of 50
kernels. A consumer watchdog group has written you a letter claiming that your company is not
including the appropriate number of kernels of popcorn per bag. As evidence, they send you
results showing that a random sample of 16 bags had a mean of 1226 kernels per bag. Using their
data, how would you phrase your research question if you want the null hypothesis to be justified
at the 95% confidence level? Show calculations comparing results for a one tail question and a
two tail question.
Problem 3 (30%) A local pizzeria offers a “Pepperoni Feast” pizza advertised to be covered
with (on average) 100 slices of pepperoni. You’ve had some time on your hands lately, so you’ve
counted the number of slices of pepperoni on each of the nine Pepperoni Feast pizzas you’ve
ordered in the past few months. You’ve collected the following series of results: 97, 105, 90, 92,
89, 92, 97, 105, 94. Based on your data, can the pizzeria’s claim that Pepperoni Feast pizzas
contain an average of 100 slices of pepperoni be disproved at the (a, 15%) 95% confidence
level? (b, 15%) 99% confidence level?
Problem 4 (20%) It is well-established that systolic blood pressure for the US population is a
normally-distributed variable centered around a mean of 120 mmHg with a standard deviation of
12 mmHg. A researcher studying inhabitants of a small town in rural North Carolina suspects the
town’s residents have a significantly lower systolic blood pressure than the US population. Data
collected from a random sample of 9 residents from the rural town demonstrates a mean systolic
blood pressure of 111 mmHg. Based on this data, can it be stated that the residents of the rural
town have a mean systolic blood pressure significantly lower than the US population at the (a,
10%) 95% confidence level? (b, 10%) 99% confidence level?
