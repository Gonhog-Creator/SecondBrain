Fundamental basis of all nuclear engineering calculations is the
Reaction Rate Equation:
Reaction Rate (1/sec) = N x s x f
where:
N = number of atoms (of a particular isotope) (# atoms)
f = neutron flux in neutrons per cm2 per second (n/cm2/sec)
s = cross section; probability that reaction will occur in barns (b) (where 1 b
= 1x10-24 cm2)

SO, in Nuclear Engineering we need to understand:
• What nuclei are neutrons reacting with?
• How are they reacting and with what probability?
• What are the products of these reactions and what forms of
matter and energy (e.g. radiation) do they produce?

Stable Isotopes:
• Grey block on top half; color of bottom half indicates magnitude of neutron s.
• Elemental Symbol and Atomic Mass Number; A = N+Z for the isotope
• Atom Percent Abundance (given for abundances in nature)
• Neutron Cross Sections in barns (thermal and resonance integrals), leading to
isomeric and ground states (see Cobalt-60 slide for good example).
• If it is a potential fission product, it is noted by presence of black triangle in lower
right corner.
