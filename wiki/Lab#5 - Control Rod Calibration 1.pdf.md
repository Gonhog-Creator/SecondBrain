ANALYSIS:
1.

The reactivity values as determined by the Inhour curve (see attached on page #3) and the Small Reactivity
Insertion One-Group Inhour approximation should be in close relative agreement (assuming reactor periods
of greater than  = 30 seconds were utilized).

2.

The DIFFERENTIAL ROD WORTH (pcm/inch) for each pull is calculated by taking the reactivity calculated in
STEP #5 above and dividing by the change in final minus initial rod heights ( inches). This value is then
plotted at the midpoint rod height for each pull.

3.

The INTEGRAL ROD WORTH (total pcm) is plotted at the endpoint (final) rod position for each withdrawal. It
is the sum of the rod worth for each withdrawal plus all previous withdrawals.
