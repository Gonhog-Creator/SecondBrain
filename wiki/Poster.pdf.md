The Nukes
Jose Barbeito, Kevin Huang, Grier Neese, Ryan Perry, Joshua Yagozinski
Abstract
This literature review focuses on neutron imaging techniques used to visualize the physical structure of lithium ion batteries in operando. Lithium ion batteries are ubiquitous, used to power many portable devices around the world such as phones, laptops, and headphones. The batteries are also used in medical devices,
drones, electric vehicles, and many other applications where high electrical power density and rechargeability are required. Because lithium ion batteries (LIBs) are so common, there is significant interest in improving their properties such as power density, safety, cost, and reliability. However, to research these
advancements, the internal structure must be analyzed in a non destructive manner while the battery is in operation. The parts of an LIB are degraded through charge cycling of the battery, resulting in problems such as dendrite growth on the lithium anodes and gas generation due to decomposition of the electrolyte and
cathode. Neutron radiography has been proven to be an effective method of monitoring these processes, as compared to conventional X-ray imaging techniques, neutrons are non destructive and capable of achieving much higher contrast imaging of the battery, as unlike X-rays, neutrons are easily attenuated by light
elements such as lithium. While neutron imaging does not have as high resolution as X-rays, it significantly increases the amount of information that can be gathered from in operando studies of LIBs.

Advantages of Neutron Imaging

Motivations for Developing Neutron Imaging
The development of neutron imaging has been motivated by the need to address challenges in the industrialization of lithium
metal electrodes with conventional liquid electrolytes, specifically when studying microscopic dendrite growth. Dendrite growth,
in the form of mosses, needles, and globules, is attributed to the leading cause of short circuits and catastrophic failures in lithium
batteries.
