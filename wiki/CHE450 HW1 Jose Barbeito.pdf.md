a) The A/B designates two pumps, a primary pump and a backup pump. We choose
to have 2 pumps because 1) pumps are cheap and 2) having the entire process
go down because of a pump failure is bad, therefore having a secondary pump to
switch to in the case of a pump failure is good practice.
b) Reactor A/B would be used when there is a lifetime for the reactor, such as a
catalyst that needs to be replaced every X days or a bed that is filled with
contaminant and needs to be replaced with new material. Having two reactors
allows you to switch from one reactor to another while you change out parts in
the old reactor, giving better uptime for the process.

space for OSHA regulations.

potential conflicts with contained chemicals or safety hazards. In addition it is
helpful to know the material for material properties, such as melting point and
thermal conductivity.

column. More than likely it is to ensure wastewater is of an acceptable
temperature, as high temperature fluids could be prone to flashing, reacting with
other waste, or corroding infrastructure downstream. In addition, the local

f) The recycle stream 13 is included in the process design so that unreacted

g) There is no overhead stream from V-202 because it is fully liquid, insinuating that
E-205 is a complete condenser and condenses all the gas from the distillation
column into a liquid.
h) In E-202, the output stream from the reactor, which is hot vapor with around 40%
water and dimethyl ether and 20% unreacted methanol, is being used to pre-heat
the incoming stream of the reactor, which has a composition of roughly all
methanol. This means that the output stream is losing heat, transferring it to the
incoming stream. Since there are no heating elements labeled in the reactor, we
can assume that the reaction is extremely exothermic. By using the output
stream of the reactor to preheat the input stream, you lower the temperature of
your output stream (which you need to do for your distillation column) and
increase the temperature of your input stream, encouraging the reaction to
proceed in the reactor.
i) There are two distillation columns to separate the product and then separate the
reactant. In T-201, the product is separated from the methanol/water/DMO
mixture. The button product is a mixture of unreacted methanol and water. In
order to separate that stream and return unreacted methanol to the process, it is
separated with another distillation column.
