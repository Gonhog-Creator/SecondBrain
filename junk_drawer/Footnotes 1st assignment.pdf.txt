Chicago Style of Footnoting:
To cite a book:
First name Last name, Title of Book (Place of publication: Publisher, Year of publication), page
number.
John Murrin, Pekka Hämäläinen, Paul Johnson, et al, Liberty, Equality, Power: A History of the
American People, Volume 2: Since 1863 (Boston: Cengage, 2019), page number. (Replace “page
number” in this example with the pages you are citing.)
To cite a website:
“Fabulous Imperialism! The 1893 Columbian Exposition,” The Pinky Show, Associated
Animals, Inc., 2019 (accessed Feb. 1, 2021),
http://www.pinkyshow.org/projectarchives/videos/fabulous-imperialism-the-1893-columbianexposition
To cite a newspaper article:
Timothy Tyson, “The Ghosts of 1898,” News & Observer (Raleigh, NC), Nov. 17 2006.
To cite a lecture:
Nancy Mitchell, “Then & Now, and the Chicago World's Fair," Lecture for HI 254-601, Spring
2024.

The 2nd time you cite a book, article, website, etc., you use a shortened form:
•
•
•

Murrin, Liberty, Equality, Power, page number.
“Fabulous Imperialism!”
Tyson, “Ghosts of 1898,” News & Observer.

If you are citing the same source consecutively, you can use “Ibid.” which means

“ditto.” It always has a period because it is an abbreviation of Ibidem.
