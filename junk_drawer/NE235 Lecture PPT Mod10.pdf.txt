Δρnet = Δρexcess+Δρrods+Δρmod temp+Δρfuel temp+Δρvoids+Δρxenon+Δρexp+Δρsource

𝝆𝒏𝒆𝒕 = 𝟎

make corrections.

Used for calculating ‘Estimated Critical Position’ (ECP) at startup to project

that reactivity parameters are sufficiently well known to allow for startup.

Reactivity Balances
Importance of Reactivity Balances:
1.

Know ECP within ±200pcm of ACP prior to startup – otherwise you
don’t know what is going on inside of reactor, so SHUTDOWN!

2.

Know your estimated critical rod position (ECP) at cold-clean-critical to
determine compliance with Technical Specifications “Limiting
Condition for Operations” (LCO) Requirements PRIOR TO STARTUP:
a) TS §3.2(b) Excess Reactivity < 3.97% ∆K/K (3970 pcm)
b) TS §3.2(a) Shutdown Margin > 0.4% ∆K/K (400 pcm)
with:
i. Highest Scrammable Rod Fully Withdrawn
ii. Shim Rod Fully Withdrawn
iii. Experiments at Their Most Reactive Condition
iv. Relative to “Cold, Clean, Critical” (Xenon free; Tmod = 70°F)

Reactivity Balances
Why have these limits?
a) Shutdown Margin: To assure that the reactor may always be
shutdown from any condition of operation and will remain shutdown
after cooldown, xenon decay, or removal of an experiment.

b) Excess Reactivity: An upper limit on excess reactivity is additional
assurance that an adequate shutdown margin is maintained. It also
limits the extent of a power excursion in the event of a transient
accident.
