Colloids Problem
Colloidal Suspension: Yunhu from the Hsiao group has been working with an
unknown colloidal particle (named BQ) supplied to him from a company. Since very
little is known about BQ except size, 0.8 microns in diameter, Yunhu is trying to
decipher its rheological behavior in various solvents (e.g., acting as a hard sphere,
have electrostatic, van der Waals, static interactions, etc).
(a) (10) A high school intern working for Yunhu mixes 0.15 volume fraction of
BQ in a Newtonian solvent of viscosity 4 Pa.s. He then measures the viscosity
and says that he found the suspension to have a viscosity of 5.5 Pa.s. However,
when Yunhu measures the same sample, he gets a viscosity of 4.75 Pa.s. Are
any of these values possible or both making mistakes in their measurements? If
one is correct, who is correct, and if one of these measurements is accurate,
can you say what is happening in terms of rheological behavior. Explain your
answer.
