Enolates
•
•

The α-H of carbonyl compounds are somewhat acidic.
The conjugate base formed by the removal of an α-H is called an
enolation ion or enolate.

Enols
•

An enol is any compound in which a hydroxy group is on a carbon of a C–C
double bond.

•

Most carbonyl compounds with α-H are in equilibrium with small amounts of
their enol isomer.
