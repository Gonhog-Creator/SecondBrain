Reactor Design
Considerations
We therefore design the reactor to be sub-critical without delayed neutrons.
Adjusting parameters such as:
• Fuel Loading – How much 235𝑈 in core + enrichment
• Fuel to Moderator Ratio – e.g. 235𝑈 𝑣𝑠 𝐻2 𝑖𝑛 𝑐𝑜𝑟𝑒
• Core Surface to Volume Ratio - Adjust leakage
• Control Rod Loading – Absorber loading & withdrawal speed
We want “sources” of neutrons to be only slightly greater than “sinks” of
neutrons so that you are subcritical without delayed neutrons.

Neutron Multiplication Cycle
If Keff is raised to be > 1: (See power trace in Fig 2.3 on next slide)

1. Initially there is a sharp rise in neutron population due to rapid multiplication of
prompt neutrons.
2. The initial rate of delayed neutron release (B) stays the same because saturated
activities (Ci) of precursor groups is based on pre-increase withholding rate (A).
This is based on flux.
• Withholding (A) builds inventory over time which leads to an increased
release (B).
• Therefore, (A) may increase immediately with prompt jump, but inventory
takes time to accumulate and to result in increased release rate (B).
• Therefore, the rate of population multiplication decreases as reactor ‘waits’
for delayed neutron release rate (B) to catch up.
3. Rate of release (B) increases as precursor group activity (Ci) increases.
For K>1.0, the rate of population increase becomes exponential.

Summary
• Prompt vs Delayed Neutron Generation Times: Delayed
neutrons are required for control of the reactor.
• Reactor Design Considerations: Adjust design parameters to
maintain reactor sub-critical without delayed neutrons.

• Fission Product Delayed Neutron Pre-Cursors: Delayed
neutrons come from decay of neutron rich fission products.
• Neutron Multiplication Cycle including Delayed Neutrons:
Delayed neutrons are stored and released at rates that are a
function of their half-lives and fission yields.
• Response to Reactivity at Beginning –vs- End of Core Life:
Reactor response to positive reactivity speeds up as core
ages due to breeding of 239Pu and depletion of 235U.
