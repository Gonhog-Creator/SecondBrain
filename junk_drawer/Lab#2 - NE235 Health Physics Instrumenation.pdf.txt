GM Detectors
пҒ°

A fill gas (typically a mixture of Argon and Methane) is
contained in a sealed tube of various shapes, sizes

пҒ°

Ionization occurs in the fill gas and in the detector wall
material (wall is at ground potential and serves as the
cathode)

пҒ°

GM detectors produce a full amplitude pulse (count) for any
ion that enters the detector (no energy distinction).

пҒ°

Used to detect alpha, beta, gamma and x-rays

пҒ°

Used primarily for contamination surveys, reading out as a
count rate (CPM).

пҒ°

May be calibrated to give approximate exposure rate
(mR/hr) information for specific type of radiation field.

Ion Chambers
пҒ°

Fill gas is air that may or may not be contained in a
sealed chamber

пҒ°

Ionization occurs in the fill gas and in the detector wall
material (wall is at ground potential and serves as the
cathode)

пҒ°

Ions in the fill gas are collected at the detector anode
and produce a pulse height proportional to the number
of interactions, or ionization, occurring in the detection
volume

пҒ°

Ion chambers are very accurate and allow distinction
between radiation types based on pulse height.

пҒ°

Used to detect beta, gamma and x-rays

пҒ°

Used to accurately measure exposure rate (mR/hr)
for external radiation fields.

Scintillation Detectors

Scintillation crystals respond to
radiation interactions by
producing light. The light is
then detected (e.g. via a Photo
Multiplier Tube - PMT) and
converted to an electrical signal
with a pulse height proportional
to the radiation energy
deposition rate.
Scintillators are extremely
sensitive to radiation and are
often used for obtaining energy
spectra.

Neutron Detectors
пҒ°

Neutrons donвҖҷt interact with electrons, so Boron
(10B) or Lithium (6Li) are utilized due to their
large thermal neutron absorption cross sections.

пҒ°

Neutron is captured by a Lithium or Boron

ionization in the detector.

пҒ°

Our Neutron вҖҳREM BallвҖҷ utilizes a BF3 (Boron TriFluoride) gas filled tube detector.

пҒ°

The polyethylene ball moderates high energy

being detected in the BF3 tube.

пҒ°

High energy neutrons have a higher вҖҳQuality
factorвҖҷ (or RBE factor) than thermal neutrons,
so result in a higher dose relative to exposure.

пҒ°

Reads out in units of mREM/hr.

пҒ°

Utilized for performing surveys of external
neutron fields around neutron sources and
beamport experiments.
