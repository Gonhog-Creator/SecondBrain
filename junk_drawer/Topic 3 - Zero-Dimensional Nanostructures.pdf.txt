Quantum Confinement
When the size of a nanocrystal approaches the de
Broglie wavelength of electrons (wave-particle duality)
in the conduction band, e- and h+ are spatially
confined, dipoles are formed and discrete electronic
energy levels arise, and shift Eg (band gap). This is
known as quantum confinement
This is most pronounced for semiconductor NPs (2-10
nm), but also observed in metals (<2 nm) and
insulators

Desirable Nanoparticle Characteristics
Small size is not the only requirement. For
any practical application, synthesis & stability
needs to be controlled in such a way that the
resulting NPs have the following
characteristics:
Identical and stable properties, which
requires:
1. Identical size of all particles, aka
monosized or uniform size distribution
2. Identical shape or morphology
3. Identical chemical composition
4. Identical crystal structure
5. Core and surface compositions must be
the same
6. Individually dispersed or monodispersed,
i.e., no agglomeration
7. If agglomeration occurs, particles must
be readily redispersible

Nanoparticle Size Analysis
Transmission electron microscopy (TEM)
вҖ“ Direct
вҖ“ High resolution ~0.1 nm (size and shape)
вҖ“ Technically demanding sample preparation and high cost

Scanning electron microscopy (SEM)
вҖ“ Direct
вҖ“ Resolution limited to >1 nm (size and shape)
вҖ“ Requires conducting samples

Scanning probe microscopy (SPM), e.g., AFM, STM
вҖ“ Direct
вҖ“ High resolution in z (<0.1 nm), low resolution in x-y
вҖ“ Tip effects, and often sample-independent

X-ray diffraction (XRD)
вҖ“ Indirect
вҖ“ Not suitable for polycrystalline, core-shell, or noncrystalline NPs, or anisotropic samples

Dynamic light scattering (DLS)
вҖ“ Indirect
вҖ“ Medium resolution (3 nm) (size only, no shape)
