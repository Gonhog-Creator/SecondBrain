Reactivity & Delayed Neutron Fraction
Reactivity: Departure from “Criticality” (Keff=1) due to mismatch between sources and sinks:
𝝆 = (𝑲𝒆𝒇𝒇 − 𝟏)/𝑲𝒆𝒇𝒇

> 0 | Super Critical

(𝑲𝒆𝒇𝒇 > 1)

= 0 | Critical (Delayed Critical)

(𝑲𝒆𝒇𝒇 = 1)

< 0 | Sub-Critical

(𝑲𝒆𝒇𝒇 < 1)

Units: ρ(∆K/K) where 0.00001 ∆K/K = 0.001% ∆K/K = 1pcm (percent milli-rho)

Delayed Neutron Fraction β:
A reasonable value for the Delayed Neutron Fraction β of total neutrons in the PULSTAR reactor is
β = 0.007 (for 235U fission). ~0.7% of fission neutrons arise from the decay of neutron rich fission
product isotopes which decay with short half lives.

Effective Delayed Neutron Fraction βeff:
Delayed neutrons are “born” at lower energies than prompt neutrons and are therefore less likely
to leak out of the reactor as fast neutrons or to be absorbed in the resonance region. This makes
them more “effective” in propagating the chain reaction. Thus, the Effective Delayed Neutron
Fraction, βeff = 0.0073 (for 235U fission).

Boundary Between Delayed and Prompt Critical:
A reactor is said to be delayed critical when production of prompt fission neutrons plus delayed
neutron precursors is just equal to the loss rate of neutrons by absorption and leakage.
