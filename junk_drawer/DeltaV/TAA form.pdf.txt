1. Instructions
Based on the initial information you have provided to NTS Technical Systems during the
RFQ process, we have determined that our testing services for you, our customer, will
require a license from the U.S. Government before we can perform the test and issue a test
report to you. This is a data-gathering form that we will need you to complete in order to
apply for the export authorization from the US Government. Please provide accurate and
detailed information to avoid having our license application rejected.
Please note that that both the testing services that NTS provides to you, and the
information contained in our test report to you are both export-controlled and require a
license or an agreement before your company can receive them. Also, discussions of test
results in emails or by phone or video conference cannot occur until the license or
agreement has been approved. Once we have an export authorization in place, we can
conduct the testing and transfer the test results to your company via our secure file-sharing
program, LabInsight. https://labinsight.nts.com/labinsight
Test items controlled by the U.S. International Trafficking in Arms Regulations (“ITAR”)
will require a Technical Assistance Agreement (“TAA”) from the U.S. Department of
State’s Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (“DDTC”) before testing can begin. Test
items controlled by the U.S. Export Administration Regulations (“EAR”) require a license
from the U.S. Department of Commerce’s Bureau of Industry and Security (“BIS”) before
the test report can be sent and the test item return to you.
Please provide the information requested, below, which NTS will use this information in
filing our license application with the government. Please note that license applications
rejected by the government for incomplete or insufficient information may cause weeks of
delay in the planned testing schedule.

4. Information on the export license process:
Licenses or Technical Assistance Agreements from the U.S. State Department will take
from 60 to 90 days to be approved. Once a Technical Assistance Agreement has been
approved, each party to the Agreement must sign the document confirming that they
understand what is permitted under the agreement. Once the Agreement is signed,
NTS will upload the Agreement to the State Department’s database, and we can begin
the testing. If your business name changes, or if we need to add additional customers
to the Agreement, please inform NTS Export immediately so that we can update the
Agreement.
Licenses from the U.S. Department of Commerce usually take 30 to 60 days for the
government to approve. No signatures are necessary once the license is approved.
